TOP DESTINATIONS

Mathura

JAMA MASJID : Built by Abo-inNabir-Khan in 1661.A.D. the mosque has 4 lofty minarets, with bright colored plaster mosaic of which a few panels currently exist. VISHRAM GHAT : The sacred spot where Lord Krishna is believed to have rested after slaying the tyrant Kansa. DWARKADHEESH TEMPLE : Built in 1814, it is the main temple in the town. During the festive days of Holi, Janmashthami and Diwali, it is decorated on a grandiose scale.

Agra

Agra Fort This is located on the bank of River Yamuna. Ever since Babur defeated and killed Ibrahim Lodi at Panipat in 1526, Agra played an important role for the Mughal Empire. Akbar chose Agra as his capital.It is said that he destroyed the damaged old fort of Agra and built a grand group of monuments instead in red sandstone.

Vrindavan

Vrindavan, 15 kms from Mathura, is another major place of pilgrimage. It is noted for its numerous temples - both old and modern. The name Vrindavan evokes the playfulness and lovable characteristics of Shri Krishna. This is the wood where he frolicked with the gopis and tenderly wooed Radha. Vrindavan today, is noted for its numerous temples.

Gokul

The most important temple in Gokul is Gokul Nath Ji. Gokul is particularly famous for the extravagant celebrations of festivals like Janmashtmi, Trinvat Mela and Annakut. In Gokul the place called 'Raman Reti' is visited by innumerable devotees who roll over the sand to ask the blessings of Lord Krishna. Gokul is also popular for the seat of Ballabhacharya, a descendant of 16th century Bhakti movement.

Rishikesh

located in the foothills of the Himalaya and attracts thousands of pilgrims and tourists each year, from within India, as well as from other countries. Rishikesh is a vegetarian city by law as well as an alcohol-free city. Rishikesh is also known as "Teerth Nagri" or "Yog Nagri" or "Yoga City", that is City of Temples, Yoga and Meditation.
