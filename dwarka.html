TOP DESTINATIONS

Somnath

The museum at Somnath houses the remains of the earlier temples but in the form a clutter of old carved stones kept in the courtyard. It also houses pottery shards, a seashell collection, a glass case of water bottles containing samples from the rivers Danube, Nile, St, Lawrence, Tigris, Murray and seawater from Tasmania and New Zealand.

Porbandar

The Huzoor Palace is a massaive structure like a European mansion, sprawling with a large garden at the edge the sea. The Palace has various wings in a zigzag formation, which created space for numerous forecourts and rear courtyards, which were used for gardens and fountains.

Dwarka

The city derives its name from word dvar meaning door and gate in the Sanskrit language. Dwarka is considered to be one of the holiest cities in Hinduism and one of the four ―dhams‖ along with Badrinath, Puri, Rameswaram. The city is especially respected by Vaishnavas.

Ahmedabad

Ahmedabad is the largest city in Gujarat State. It is the seventh largest city and eighth largest metropolitan area. Ahmedabad is the fastest growing city of India, and ranked third as the fastest growing city worldwide.The 3 most popular and most visited gardens in the city are Victoria Garden, Bal Vatika and Law Garden. Located at the southern verge of the Bhadra Fort, Victoria Garden is one of the most popular places in the city. The Victoria Garden has a statue of Queen Victoria which is a masterpiecein itself.
